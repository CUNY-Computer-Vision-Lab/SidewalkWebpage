Introduction

This codebook is meant to be used as a guide for how to use Project Sidewalk to label accessibility problems in a standardized way, that is grounded in a close reading of the specifications of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and related documents. The codebook contains instructions for where to place labels, how to rate severity of the labels, along with rationale for those decisions (including ADA citations where appropriate).

Important note on which pano to label from: Always label an issue/feature from the place where it is most visible or closest (being closer to the thing you label makes the lat-lng of the label more accurate).

Important note on label placement: Place labels at the center of curb ramp or surface problem, and at the base of an obstacle. This is both to standardize the placement and, in the case of obstacles, making sure that the location is accurate. Placing a label higher than the base of an obstacle could give a very inaccurate lat-lng. The left and center images show correct placement, and the right image shows incorrect placement.

Important note on temporal change between adjacent panos: If you take a step and things change (temporary obstacles like construction are removed, a curb ramp has been added or improved, etc), then label each panorama appropriately. When doing this, also click the feedback button and write a message saying what changed between the 2 panos. You do not need to label the same problem from multiple panoramas if nothing changes.
