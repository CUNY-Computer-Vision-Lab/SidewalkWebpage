Welcome!

We are looking for workers who can label the streets of Washington D.C. for us. We are particularly interested in identifying:

  • accessibility features like Curb Ramps at intersections and the absence of them ("Missing Curb Ramp");
  • problems such as degraded sidewalk ("Surface Problem"), trash bins, light poles and others in the middle of a sidewalk ("Obstacle in Path") and absence of sidewalk ("No Sidewalk").

We have a fun interface (see the image) that allows you to walk virtually through the neighborhoods of DC in Google Street View to find and label. To complete this HIT, you need to do the following:

  • Step 1: Go through a short tutorial to learn how to use the interface. You only need to do it once for the HIT in this group.
  • Step 2: Complete a "mission" of walking and finding accessibility features and problems within a neighborhood.
  • Step 3: When you finish the "mission", we will show a complete mission dialog where you click the SUBMIT HIT button. Note, the submit button shows up only when you complete the mission.

Please press the ACCEPT HIT button to get started.
